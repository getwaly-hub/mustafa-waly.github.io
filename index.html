How I Think

اللي بيقتل الـ conversion مش المنتج — هو الجملة الأولى
01 Pattern Interrupt The first job is to break the scroll. Not with noise — with recognition. The viewer has to see themselves before they see the product. بتعمل routine الـ skincare من 6 شهور. بشرتك لسه زي ما هي.
02 Identity Signal The hook doesn't describe a person. It inhabits their specific frustration. Not "do you have dry skin?" — but "you've been doing everything right and nothing changed." مش كسلان. مش مش عارف. — بيعمل كل حاجة صح.
03 Stakes Statement After recognition, the reader needs to feel what's at risk. Not fear. Not urgency. The quiet cost of staying where they are. كل يوم بيعدي، الفرصة بتقل.
04 Open Loop The sentence ends before it resolves. The brain craves closure. It has to know what happens next — and that means it keeps watching. في سبب واحد بس بيوقف النتيجة —

This isn't a creative exercise. This is the audit I run on every brief — before I write a single word. I check which of these four elements is missing. That's where the conversion is leaking. Same framework. Every niche. Different knife.

Publishing daily. Healthy views. Real saves. One problem: none of it was converting strangers into buyers. The content was working — the first sentence wasn't.

The Gap
The creative was solid. The targeting was right. The budget was running. But the CTR was stuck at 0.9% — cold audience, no trust, no reason to stop.
The Real Problem
The first sentence was describing the product. Not the person. It was selling before it was seen. Cold buyers don't convert when they feel sold to — they convert when they feel understood.
The Structural Fix
Rebuilt the hook architecture using the 4-part anatomy: Pattern Interrupt → Identity Signal → Stakes → Open Loop. No new budget. No new targeting. A different first sentence.
